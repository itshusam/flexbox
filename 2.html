About Linkin Park

Linkin Park is an American rock band formed in Agoura Hills, California, in 1996. The band rose to international fame with their debut album, Hybrid Theory (2000), which was certified Diamond by the RIAA in 2005 and multi-Platinum in several other countries. Their following studio albums continued to explore different musical styles, incorporating alternative rock, electronic music, and hip hop.

Linkin Park's members are:

  • Mike Shinoda
  • Brad Delson
  • Rob Bourdon
  • Joe Hahn
  • Dave "Phoenix" Farrell

The band's distinctive style and sound have earned them numerous awards and accolades, making them one of the best-selling bands of the 21st century.
